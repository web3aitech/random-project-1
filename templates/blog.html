How Often Should Hotels Deep Clean? A Complete Frequency Guide

The definitive guide for hotel directors of housekeeping — covering every surface type and how often restorative cleaning is required.

Published June 2026 · 7 min read · Hotel Operations Housekeeping

Why deep cleaning frequency matters more than most GMs think

Routine daily housekeeping — vacuuming, mopping, surface wipe-downs — is essential but insufficient. Over time, every surface in your hotel accumulates embedded soil, body oils, allergens, and microorganisms that routine cleaning simply cannot address.

The consequences are real: degraded carpet fiber life, discolored tile grout, etched marble surfaces, and PTAC units blowing contaminated air through guest rooms. These all contribute to lower guest review scores, earlier asset replacement, and ultimately lost revenue.

Here's the frequency schedule our technicians recommend after servicing thousands of hotel properties.

Carpet cleaning

High-traffic areas (corridors, lobby, meeting rooms): every 3–4 months

Guest rooms: every 6–12 months, depending on occupancy rate

Ballrooms and event spaces: after major events + quarterly preventive

Carpet is the single highest-impact surface for guest perception. A clean, fresh-smelling carpet immediately signals quality. An odorous or visibly soiled carpet — regardless of how clean everything else is — drives negative reviews.

Tile & grout restoration

Lobby and public restrooms: every 6 months

Fitness centers and pool areas: every 4–6 months (higher moisture = faster soil buildup)

Kitchen tile (behind prep surfaces): every 3–4 months

Grout is porous and discolors faster than the surrounding tile. Once grout turns dark, it creates a negative visual even when the tile itself is clean. Restoration is always more cost-effective than re-grouting.

Marble & stone restoration

Lobby floors and feature walls: annually

Bathroom vanities and floors: every 12–18 months

Marble is susceptible to acidic etching from cleaning products, body products, and even food spills. Polishing and sealing annually maintains the reflective finish that makes luxury hotel lobbies look the part.

PTAC / VTAC cleaning

All guest room units: every 6–12 months

PTAC coils accumulate dust, pet dander, and biological matter that gets blown into guest rooms continuously. This is a common source of guest complaints about musty odors — and one of the easiest problems to solve proactively.

Commercial kitchen cleaning

Hood, vent systems, and deep surfaces: every 3 months (required by most health codes)

Tile floors, walls, and equipment exteriors: monthly deep clean

Creating your annual deep clean calendar

The most effective approach is to build a 12-month calendar at the start of each year, aligned with your occupancy forecast. Schedule deep cleans during historically lower-occupancy periods when room access is easier and guest disruption is minimized.
